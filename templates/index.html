Research

Development of Algae and Turbidity Indices Using LANDSAT Scenes
Due to increased fertilizer usage in recent years, the issue of harmful algal blooms has become increasingly more important for states with large agricultural sectors. Typically the concentration of these harmful algal blooms are assesed by sending a boat out and placing a sensor into the lake at various points. Using machine learning techniques on 3 years of in-situ and LANDSAT data, this research seeks to develop remotely sensed indices for the presence of algae and turbidity in Grand Lake O' the Cherokees.
Updating PyHSPF
The PyHSPF module principally developed by Dr. David Lampert seeks to automate the data acquisition, and formatting of fortran input files for the HSPF model (Hydrologic Simulation Program Fortran). The data needed for the HSPF model comes from various sources around the web including NWIS, USGS, and the EPA. These sources constantly change the way their data is hosted. I have worked on standardizing the data acquisitions from these various sources.
Web Scraper for the National Inventory of Dams
The Army Core of Engineers hosts the National Inventory of Dams. This database holds lots of pertinent information about dams and reservoirs in the United States. The problem with their website is that the data has no api accespoints, or downloadable data. They only present the NID database in a javascript generated table. I wrote a Selenium test to scrape the NID website for all 90,000+ rows. You can view the data as .csv by state here . All credit to the Army Core of Engineers for the data.
